## 1. Services

### 1.1 General Scope
Contractor agrees to provide software development and consulting services as described in individual Statements of Work ("SOW") executed under this Agreement.

### 1.2 Statements of Work
Specific projects will be detailed in separate SOWs which will reference this Master Agreement. Each SOW will specify:
- Detailed scope and deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Fees and payment terms
- Any project-specific terms

### 1.3 Independent Contractor
Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee. Contractor:
- Controls means and methods of work
- Provides own tools and equipment
- Is responsible for own taxes and insurance
- May serve other clients concurrently

---

## 2. Compensation & Payment

### 2.1 Fees
Fees will be specified in each SOW. Standard hourly rate is $[Rate]/hour unless otherwise specified.

### 2.2 Invoicing
Contractor will invoice Client [frequency - typically bi-weekly or monthly]. Invoices will detail:
- Description of services performed
- Hours worked (for time-based engagements)
- Applicable SOW reference

### 2.3 Payment Terms
Payment is due within **[X days - typically 15-30]** of invoice date.

### 2.4 Late Payment
Late payments accrue interest at **1.5% per month** (18% annually) or the maximum allowed by law, whichever is less.

### 2.5 Expenses
Pre-approved expenses will be reimbursed at cost with receipts. Expenses requiring pre-approval include:
- Software licenses/subscriptions
- Cloud infrastructure costs
- Travel expenses
- Other third-party services

---

## 3. Term & Termination

### 3.1 Term
This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either party.

### 3.2 Termination for Convenience
Either party may terminate this Agreement with **[X days]** written notice. Individual SOWs may specify different termination terms.

### 3.3 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate immediately if:
- The other party materially breaches this Agreement
- The other party becomes insolvent or files bankruptcy
- Required by law

### 3.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
- Client pays for all work completed through termination date
- Contractor delivers all work-in-progress
- Both parties return confidential information
- Provisions that by their nature should survive will survive

---

## 4. Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Work Product
All custom work product created specifically for Client under this Agreement ("Deliverables") will be owned by Client upon full payment.

### 4.2 Pre-Existing Materials
Contractor retains ownership of:
- Pre-existing code, tools, and methodologies
- General knowledge and skills
- Reusable components and frameworks

### 4.3 License
Contractor grants Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, worldwide license to use any pre-existing materials incorporated into Deliverables.

### 4.4 Third-Party Components
Deliverables may include open-source or third-party components subject to their respective licenses. Contractor will document all such components.

### 4.5 Portfolio Rights
Contractor may use project as case study and portfolio piece with Client's prior written approval (not to be unreasonably withheld).

---

## 5. Confidentiality

### 5.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" includes:
- Trade secrets, source code, algorithms
- Business plans, financial information
- Customer lists and data
- Any information marked confidential
- Information that should reasonably be considered confidential

### 5.2 Obligations
Each party agrees to:
- Keep confidential information private
- Use it only for purposes of this Agreement
- Protect it with same care as own confidential information (but at least reasonable care)
- Not disclose without prior written consent

### 5.3 Exceptions
Does not apply to information that:
- Was publicly known before disclosure
- Becomes public through no fault of receiving party
- Was independently developed
- Was rightfully received from a third party
- Must be disclosed by law (with notice if possible)

### 5.4 Duration
Obligations survive for **[2-5] years** after termination of this Agreement.

## 7. Limitation of Liability

### 7.1 Cap on Liability
**CONTRACTOR'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IS LIMITED TO THE AMOUNT PAID BY CLIENT IN THE 12 MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.**

### 7.2 Excluded Damages
**NEITHER PARTY IS LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THEIR POSSIBILITY.**

### 7.3 Exceptions
Limitations do not apply to:
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Intellectual property infringement
- Confidentiality breaches
- Matters that cannot be limited by law

---

## 8. Indemnification

### 8.1 By Contractor
Contractor will indemnify Client against claims that Deliverables infringe third-party intellectual property rights, provided Client:
- Promptly notifies Contractor of claim
- Gives Contractor control of defense
- Cooperates in defense

### 8.2 By Client
Client will indemnify Contractor against claims arising from:
- Client's use of Deliverables beyond scope of license
- Modifications to Deliverables made by Client
- Client's content or data

---

## 9. General Provisions

### 9.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement and any SOWs constitute the entire agreement and supersede all prior agreements.

### 9.2 Amendments
Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

### 9.3 Assignment
Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's written consent, except to a successor in interest through merger or acquisition.

### 9.4 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of **[Your State]**, excluding conflict of law provisions.

### 9.5 Dispute Resolution
Disputes will be resolved through:
1. Good faith negotiation
2. Mediation (if negotiation fails)
3. Binding arbitration in **[Your City, State]** (if mediation fails)

### 9.6 Attorney's Fees
Prevailing party in any dispute is entitled to reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

### 9.7 Notices
Notices must be in writing and sent to addresses above, or to email addresses provided by parties.

### 9.8 Severability
If any provision is invalid, the rest of the Agreement remains in effect.

### 9.9 Waiver
Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver of that provision.

### 9.10 Force Majeure
Neither party is liable for delays caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control.

### 9.11 Insurance
Contractor maintains:
- Professional liability insurance: $[Amount]
- General liability insurance: $[Amount]

### 9.12 Data Protection
Both parties will comply with applicable data protection laws, including GDPR and CCPA where applicable.
